_**Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood**_ ( [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language "Japanese language"): 鋼の錬金術師 FULLMETAL ALCHEMIST, [Hepburn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hepburn_romanization "Hepburn romanization"): _Hagane no Renkinjutsushi_) is a Japanese [anime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anime "Anime") television series, serving as the second animated adaptation of the [manga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manga "Manga") series _[Fullmetal Alchemist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist "Fullmetal Alchemist")_ by [Hiromu Arakawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hiromu_Arakawa "Hiromu Arakawa"), after the [2003 anime series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist_(TV_series) "Fullmetal Alchemist (TV series)"). Produced by [Bones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bones_(studio) "Bones (studio)"), the series was directed by [Yasuhiro Irie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yasuhiro_Irie "Yasuhiro Irie") and written by [Hiroshi Ōnogi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hiroshi_%C5%8Cnogi "Hiroshi Ōnogi"), with music composed by [Akira Senju](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akira_Senju "Akira Senju"). It was conceived to create a faithful adaptation that directly follows the entire story from the original manga, as the first anime adaptation strayed away from it to create a story exclusive to that series after running out of published manga material to adapt.

Production for _Brotherhood_ began in 2008. The series aired a total of 64 episodes on [MBS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainichi_Broadcasting_System "Mainichi Broadcasting System"), [TBS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TBS_Television_(Japan) "TBS Television (Japan)") and its [affiliates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan_News_Network#List_of_affiliates "Japan News Network") from April 2009 to July 2010. It was originally licensed in North America by [Funimation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funimation "Funimation") and broadcast English dubbed on [Adult Swim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adult_Swim "Adult Swim") in the United States from February 2010 to September 2011. In March 2016, Funimation's license for the series expired and it was transferred over to [Aniplex of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aniplex_of_America "Aniplex of America").

_Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood_ has received acclaim since its initial airing and is regarded as one of the [greatest anime series of all time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_anime_series_considered_the_best "List of anime series considered the best"), with praise given to its faithfulness to the manga and its incorporation of characters and plot details that were not present in the 2003 anime series, either due to unpublished manga material or creative changes to accommodate the former; the climactic episodes were also lauded for both their action sequences and moral messaging.

## Plot

See also: [_Fullmetal Alchemist_ § Plot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist#Plot "Fullmetal Alchemist")

Brothers [Edward](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Elric "Edward Elric") and [Alphonse Elric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alphonse_Elric "Alphonse Elric") are raised by their mother Trisha Elric in the remote village of Resembool in the country of Amestris. Their father Hohenheim, a noted and very gifted alchemist, abandoned his family while the boys were still young. While in Trisha's care, Ed and Al began to show an affinity for alchemy and became curious about its secrets. However, when Trisha died of a lingering illness, they were cared for by their elderly neighbor Pinako Rockwell and her granddaughter [Winry Rockbell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Winry_Rockbell "Winry Rockbell"), the boys' closest friend. Ed and Al traveled the world to advance their alchemic training under teacher Izumi Curtis. Upon returning home, the brothers desperately decide to try to bring their mother back to life with alchemy. However, human transmutation is a serious alchemical taboo, as it is impossible to do so properly. In the failed transmutation, Al's body is completely disintegrated, and Ed loses his left leg. In a last attempt to keep his brother alive, Ed sacrifices his right arm to return Al's soul and binds it to a nearby suit of armor. After Edward receives automail prosthetics from Winry and Pinako, the brothers burn down their house, symbolizing their determination and decision of "no turning back", and head to the capital city to become government-sanctioned State Alchemists. After passing the exam, Edward is dubbed the "Fullmetal Alchemist" by the State Military, and the brothers begin their quest to regain their full bodies back through the fabled [Philosopher's Stone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosopher's_Stone "Philosopher's Stone") under the direction of Colonel [Roy Mustang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roy_Mustang "Roy Mustang"). Along the way, they discover a deep government conspiracy to hide the true nature of the Philosopher's Stone that involves artificially-made humans known as [homunculi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Fullmetal_Alchemist_characters#Homunculi "List of Fullmetal Alchemist characters"), the alchemists of the neighboring nation of Xing, a [scarred serial killer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scar_(Fullmetal_Alchemist) "Scar (Fullmetal Alchemist)") from the war-torn nation of Ishval, and their own father's past.

## Production

In the 20th volume of the _[Fullmetal Alchemist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist "Fullmetal Alchemist")_ [manga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manga "Manga"), released in August 2008, series author [Hiromu Arakawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hiromu_Arakawa "Hiromu Arakawa") announced that a second [anime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anime "Anime") adaptation was being produced; the previous series, _[Fullmetal Alchemist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist_(TV_series) "Fullmetal Alchemist (TV series)")_, had debuted in 2003 and featured an anime original story direction midway through its run after exhausting manga content to adapt. The Japanese title of the second adaptation is _Hagane no Renkinjutsushi: Fullmetal Alchemist_ (鋼の錬金術師 FULLMETAL ALCHEMIST; abbreviated as 鋼の錬金術師FA), including the English translation as a subtitle to differentiate it from the 2003 series,[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-animage200810-14)[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-ANN_confirmed-15)[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-storyenglishrel-16) while the English release uses the subtitle _Brotherhood_.

[Bones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bones_(studio) "Bones (studio)") produced and animated _Brotherhood_ with [Yasuhiro Irie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yasuhiro_Irie "Yasuhiro Irie") serving as director, [Hiroshi Ōnogi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hiroshi_%C5%8Cnogi "Hiroshi Ōnogi") as writer,[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-animage200810-14)[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-ANN_confirmed-15) and [Akira Senju](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akira_Senju "Akira Senju") as composer.[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-CDJapan-17) Voice actresses [Romi Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romi_Park "Romi Park") and [Rie Kugimiya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rie_Kugimiya "Rie Kugimiya") reprised their roles as Edward and Alphonse Elric, respectively.[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-18) Unlike the first adaptation, _Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood_ faithfully follows the complete story of the manga. Irie faced the difficulties of making the series as appealing as possible, and due to his large popularity within the fanbase, Irie had to properly focus on the character of [Roy Mustang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roy_Mustang "Roy Mustang").[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-19) When the manga was nearing its completion in May 2010, Irie announced that the Bones staff was already working on the final episodes adapting the ending and expressed shock at the series' conclusion.[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-20)

_Brotherhood_'s music was composed by [Akira Senju](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akira_Senju "Akira Senju").[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-CDJapan2-21) The first CD soundtrack from the anime was released on October 14, 2009.[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-CDJapan2-21) The second CD soundtrack was released on March 24, 2010.[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-22) The third CD soundtrack became available on July 7, 2010.[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-23) _Fullmetal Alchemist Final Best_, a compilation of opening and ending songs, was released on July 28, 2010.[\[23\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-24) On June 29, 2011, the original soundtrack of _Fullmetal Alchemist: The Sacred Star of Milos_ ( _FULLMETAL ALCHEMIST Nageki no Oka no Seinaru Hoshi_), composed by [Taro Iwashiro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taro_Iwashiro "Taro Iwashiro"), was released.[\[24\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-25)

## Release

Main article: [List of _Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood_ episodes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood_episodes "List of Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood episodes")

In March 2009, it was announced that the official English title of the series was _Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood_, and that it would receive its English-language premiere on [Animax Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animax_(Asian_TV_channel) "Animax (Asian TV channel)"), with Japanese audio and English subtitles, at 8:30p.m. on April 10, 2009.[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-ann-animax-27) On April 3, 2009, [Funimation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funimation "Funimation") announced it would stream English-subtitled episodes four days after they aired in Japan. Madman Entertainment would also stream it "within days" of the episodes airing in Japan.[\[27\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-28) The series premiered in Japan on April 5, 2009, on [MBS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainichi_Broadcasting_System "Mainichi Broadcasting System")– [TBS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System_Television "Tokyo Broadcasting System Television")'s Sunday 5:00 pm [JST](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_Standard_Time "Japanese Standard Time") anime block.

In May 2009, Funimation suspended the release of new episodes for a few weeks because of an incident in which an episode of _[One Piece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_Piece "One Piece")_ was uploaded before it had aired in Japan.[\[28\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-29) However, the episodes were later made available on the Funimation website and on the official Funimation channel on YouTube.[\[29\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-30)

In August 2009, Aniplex began releasing the series on [Blu-ray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blu-ray "Blu-ray") and [DVD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DVD "DVD") volumes; the first release included two episodes and an [original video animation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Original_video_animation "Original video animation") (OVA).[\[30\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-ova-31) Two more OVAs were included in the fifth and ninth volumes alongside four episodes. Other volumes feature four episodes and no OVAs. Sixteen volumes were released, the last one in November 2010.[\[31\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-ova2-32) In September 2009, Funimation announced the cast for an English dub of the series, which featured much of the cast of the 2003 series dub reprising their roles.[\[32\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-33) On February 14, 2010, the English dub of the series premiered on [Adult Swim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adult_Swim "Adult Swim")'s [Toonami](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toonami "Toonami") programming block and ended its run on September 25, 2011.[\[33\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-34)[\[34\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-eighthchedule-35) Funimation began releasing the episodes on Blu-ray and DVD in May 2010; each release contained thirteen episodes.[\[35\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-36)[\[36\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-37) Five volumes were released, with the last one in August 2011.[\[37\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-38)[\[38\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-39) In the United Kingdom, [Manga Entertainment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manga_Entertainment "Manga Entertainment") released the series in five DVD and Blu-ray volumes during 2010 and 2011,[\[39\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-40)[\[40\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-41) and later in a two-part box set.[\[41\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-42)

Funimation lost the rights to the series in March 2016.[\[42\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-43)[\[43\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-44) In July 2016, it was announced that the series' rights were transferred to [Aniplex of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aniplex_of_America "Aniplex of America") and that it would be streamed on [Crunchyroll](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crunchyroll "Crunchyroll").[\[44\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-45) [Netflix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netflix "Netflix") debuted the series in the United States and Canada in January 2018.[\[45\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-46) Aniplex of America released the series on two Blu-ray Disc box sets in November 2018.[\[46\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-47) In November 2019, Funimation re-licensed the series for its streaming service.[\[47\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-48) [Madman Entertainment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madman_Entertainment "Madman Entertainment") distributed the series in Australia;[\[48\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-49)[\[49\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-50) it was broadcast in Canada on [Super Channel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Channel_(Canadian_TV_channel) "Super Channel (Canadian TV channel)")[\[50\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-51) and in the Philippines on [TV5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TV5_(Philippine_TV_network) "TV5 (Philippine TV network)")'s AniMEGA anime programming block.[\[51\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-52)

## Reception

_Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood_ received near-universal acclaim and is widely considered to be one of the [greatest anime of all time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_anime_series_considered_the_best "List of anime series considered the best").[\[52\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-53)[\[53\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-54)[\[54\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-55) On [review aggregator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Review_aggregator "Review aggregator") site [Rotten Tomatoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes "Rotten Tomatoes"), the series holds an approval rating of 100% based on 14 reviews, with an average rating of 8/10. The site's critic consensus reads: "With impeccable world-building, rollicking action, and emotionally intelligent themes, all the elements come together to make this _Fullmetal Alchemist_ reboot a pristine distillation of the [shounen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dnen_manga "Shōnen manga") genre."[\[55\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-56) It was the top-rated anime on [MyAnimeList](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MyAnimeList "MyAnimeList"), [Anime News Network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anime_News_Network "Anime News Network") and [IMDb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IMDb "IMDb"),[\[56\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-57) until being overtaken by _[Frieren](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frieren "Frieren")_ on MyAnimeList and Anime News Network.[\[57\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-58)[\[58\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-59)

D. F. Smith, writing for _[IGN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IGN "IGN")_, noted the short time-span between the releases of the 2003 series and _Brotherhood_, which he deemed peerless among other anime.[\[59\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-IGN_DVD_1-60) Smith judged the pace of the first 14 episodes to be quicker than their original-series counterparts, which he interpreted as the series presuming the viewer is already familiar with the characters.[\[59\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-IGN_DVD_1-60) Smith also observed that while this approach enables reaching the more "exciting" story elements faster, it does exclude enjoyable moments with supporting characters, such as Winry and Lieutenant Colonel Hughes, found in the first anime series adaptation.[\[59\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-IGN_DVD_1-60)

The first 14 episodes of _Brotherhood_ were initially criticized by members of the [Anime News Network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anime_News_Network "Anime News Network") staff, who said that repeating events from the first anime series led to a lack of suspense.[\[60\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-61) Mania Entertainment's Chris Beveridge said that the entertainment in these episodes lay in the differences in the characters' actions from the first anime series, and original content which focused on the emotional themes of the series.[\[61\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-62) Beveridge praised the new fight scenes and the extra drama, which made these episodes "solid",[\[62\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-63) while Smith found the action scenes unexpectedly "compact".[\[59\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-IGN_DVD_1-60)

Megan Gudeman of [CBR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comic_Book_Resources "Comic Book Resources") called the relationships in the anime "dynamic and memorable".[\[63\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-64) Chris Zimmerman from Comic Book Bin said the series "turns around and establishes its own identity" because of the inclusion of new characters and revelations absent from the first anime series, increasing its depth. He said the animation was superior to that of the first anime series; his comments focused on the characters' expressions and the execution of the fight scenes.[\[64\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-65) Critics praised the conclusion, finding it satisfying; Mark Thomas of _The Fandom Post_ called it a "virtually perfect ending to an outstanding series".[\[65\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-66)[\[66\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-67) Writing for the _[Los Angeles Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times "Los Angeles Times")_, Charles Solomon ranked _Brotherhood_ the second-best anime of 2010 on his "Top 10" list.[\[67\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-68)

## Spin-off film

Main article: [_Fullmetal Alchemist: The Sacred Star of Milos_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_The_Sacred_Star_of_Milos "Fullmetal Alchemist: The Sacred Star of Milos")

Following the final episode of _Brotherhood_, a new film was announced.[\[68\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-69) Titled _[Fullmetal Alchemist: The Sacred Star of Milos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_The_Sacred_Star_of_Milos "Fullmetal Alchemist: The Sacred Star of Milos")_, the film was directed by Kazuya Murata and written by Yūichi Shinpo and premiered in Japanese theaters on July 2, 2011.[\[69\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-70)[\[70\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-71) The film follows the Elrics' attempts to capture a criminal in another country.[\[71\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-72) Funimation licensed the film and released it in selected theaters in the United States in January 2012, and on DVD and Blu-ray on April 24 of that same year.[\[72\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-73)[\[73\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fullmetal_Alchemist:_Brotherhood#cite_note-74)
